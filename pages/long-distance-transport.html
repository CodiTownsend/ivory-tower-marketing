FAQ

Long Distance Pet Transport FAQ

We drive approximately 500–600 miles per day to ensure your pet has adequate rest stops and isn't overtired. Houston to Los Angeles is roughly 3 days. Houston to New York is approximately 4 days. We give you a precise ETA when you book, and update you throughout the journey.

For safety during driving, pets are secured in their crate or a safety harness in the vehicle. However, during rest stops and breaks, your pet gets out-of-crate time, walks, water, and plenty of affection from our handlers. We never leave pets confined for extended periods without breaks.

You'll need current vaccination records, a health certificate from your vet (required for interstate travel), your pet's regular food (we continue their normal diet to minimize tummy upset), any medications they need, and their favorite comfort item. We walk you through a complete checklist during your consultation.

Our drivers stay at pet-friendly accommodations, and your pet stays with them — never alone in the vehicle overnight. Your pet sleeps in a comfortable, familiar environment with their handler. We never leave pets unattended in vehicles at any time.
